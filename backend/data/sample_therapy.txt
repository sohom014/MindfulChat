Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) Core Principles:
1. Cognitive Restructuring: This technique involves identifying and challenging negative or irrational thoughts (cognitive distortions). When a patient experiences anxiety, the therapist helps them recognize the "catastrophizing" thought. The patient learns to replace "If I fail this test, my life is over" with "Failing this test would be difficult, but I have options and it doesn't define my worth."
2. Grounding Techniques for Panic Attacks: The 5-4-3-2-1 technique is highly effective for sudden panic. It requires the patient to acknowledge 5 things they see, 4 things they can touch, 3 things they hear, 2 things they can smell, and 1 thing they can taste. This brings the focus back to the present physical reality and away from the internal panic spiral.
3. Behavior Activation: Often used for depression, this involves scheduling rewarding and meaningful activities. Patients suffering from depression tend to withdraw. By scheduling small tasks like taking a 10-minute walk or calling a friend, the patient can build momentum and interrupt the cycle of depressive lethargy.
